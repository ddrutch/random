About Riverbend Gazette

Our History

Since 1986, the Riverbend Gazette has been the heart of our community's information network. What started as a small family-run print newspaper has grown into a trusted source of hyperlocal news, connecting neighbors and keeping our community informed about the issues that matter most.

For nearly four decades, we have been committed to honest, accurate reporting that strengthens the bonds within our community. From covering local government meetings to celebrating student achievements, we believe that local news is the foundation of a strong democracy.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide trustworthy, hyperlocal reporting that strengthens community ties and keeps residents informed about the news and events that directly impact their daily lives.

We are committed to:

  • Accurate and unbiased reporting
  • Supporting local businesses and organizations
  • Celebrating community achievements
  • Holding local leaders accountable
  • Fostering civic engagement

Our Vision

We envision a sustainable hybrid newsroom that combines traditional local reporting with innovative digital approaches. By embracing new technologies while maintaining our commitment to quality journalism, we aim to serve both current residents and connect former community members who want to stay informed about their hometown.

Meet Our Team

Margaret Thompson - Editor-in-Chief

Margaret has been with the Gazette since 1992 and became Editor-in-Chief in 2005. She oversees all editorial content and has won several regional journalism awards for her investigative reporting on local government.

James Mitchell - Managing Editor

James joined the team in 2010 and specializes in community events and local business coverage. He is also responsible for our digital transition and website management.

Sarah Rodriguez - Sports Reporter

Sarah covers all local sports, from high school teams to community leagues. A former athlete herself, she brings passion and expertise to our sports coverage.
