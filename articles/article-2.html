New Community Center Opens Downtown

By: Mike Johnson | Published: August 22, 2025 | Category: Community News

The new Riverbend Community Center officially opened its doors to the public on Saturday. Photo by Tom Rodriguez.

After three years of planning, fundraising, and construction, the long-awaited Riverbend Community Center officially opened its doors to the public on Saturday morning, marking a significant milestone for the growing downtown area.

The 15,000 square foot facility, located at the corner of Main Street and River Avenue, represents a $2.8 million investment in the community's future and is expected to serve residents of all ages with a wide variety of programs and services.

Grand Opening Celebration

Mayor Susan Davis cut the ceremonial ribbon at 10:00 AM Saturday, joined by city council members, project donors, and hundreds of excited residents who had been eagerly awaiting the center's completion.

"This community center represents more than just a building," Mayor Davis said during her opening remarks. "It represents our commitment to bringing people together, supporting families, and investing in the future of Riverbend."

The celebration continued throughout the day with free tours, demonstrations of available programs, and activities for children including face painting and balloon animals.

Facilities and Programs

The community center features several key areas designed to serve different community needs:

Gymnasium: A full-size basketball court that can also accommodate volleyball, badminton, and other indoor sports. The gym will host youth leagues, adult recreation programs, and community events.

Meeting Rooms: Four multipurpose rooms of varying sizes available for rent by community groups, businesses, and private events. These rooms come equipped with audio-visual equipment and can accommodate groups from 10 to 100 people.

Computer Lab: A 20-station computer lab offering public internet access, computer classes, and digital literacy programs. The lab will be particularly valuable for job seekers and students who need technology access.

Senior Center: A dedicated space for older adults featuring comfortable seating areas, a small kitchen, and programming specifically designed for seniors including exercise classes, social activities, and educational workshops.

Children's Activity Room: A colorful, safe space designed for younger children with age-appropriate toys, books, and play equipment.

Programming and Activities

Community Center Director Lisa Martinez outlined an ambitious schedule of programming that will begin in September:

"We'll offer something for everyone," Martinez explained. "From fitness classes and youth sports to senior programs and business workshops, our goal is to be a true community hub."

Planned programs include:

  • After-school programs for elementary and middle school students
  • Adult fitness classes including yoga, aerobics, and weight training
  • Senior citizen activities including card games, book clubs, and health seminars
  • Youth sports leagues for various age groups
  • Computer and job skills training
  • Community meeting space for local organizations

Community Support

The project was made possible through a combination of federal grants, city funding, and community donations. Local businesses and residents contributed over $800,000 toward the project through fundraising events and direct donations.

"The level of community support has been incredible," said City Manager David Brown. "This truly was a community effort, and the center belongs to all of us."

Major donors include the Riverbend Community Foundation, First National Bank, Wilson Construction Company, and numerous local families who contributed both money and volunteer labor during construction.

Economic Impact

Beyond serving community needs, the center is expected to have a positive economic impact on the downtown area. The facility will employ 12 full-time and part-time staff members and is expected to bring additional foot traffic to nearby businesses.

"We're already seeing increased interest from people wanting to visit downtown," said Jane Wilson, owner of Wilson's Caf√© across the street. "The center is going to be great for all the local businesses."

Next Steps

Registration for fall programs begins September 1st and can be completed online at the city's website or in person at the community center. Most programs are free or offered at low cost to ensure accessibility for all residents.

The center's regular hours are Monday through Friday 6:00 AM to 10:00 PM, Saturday 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM, and Sunday 12:00 PM to 6:00 PM.

For more information about available programs and rental opportunities, contact the Community Center at (011) 555-0130 or visit the facility at 150 Main Street.
